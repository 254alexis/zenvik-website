# Homepage Conversion Strategy

## Primary Goal
The homepage should position Zenvik Technologies as a broad technology partner, not only a hosting provider.

It should convert visitors into:
- Software development leads
- Website and ecommerce clients
- Hosting and cloud customers
- Marketing and branding clients
- ICT service enquiries
- AI service enquiries
- Long-term business relationships

## Strategic Position
The homepage belongs to `zenviktechnologies.com`, the main marketing and sales website. It should help visitors discover Zenvik's capabilities and move into deeper service, pricing, contact, or portal journeys.

The WHMCS portal should be linked only where account access, checkout, invoices, tickets, or customer care are needed.

The portal homepage must never become a condensed version of the main website. Any portal cross-marketing should be lightweight and should point users back to the main website for full service discovery.

The main website is the design authority. Portal work should inspect and reuse the main-site's typography, spacing, buttons, shadows, border radius, colours, animations, and component styling instead of redesigning components that already exist here.

## Service Balance
Do not make hosting plans the dominant homepage focus.

The homepage must stay broad across:
- Software
- Websites and ecommerce
- Hosting and cloud
- Domains and SSL
- Marketing and branding
- ICT services
- AI services

Hosting can be visible and commercially important, but it should appear as part of a complete technology ecosystem rather than the single center of the homepage.

## Structure Principles
- Avoid a predictable "all services in one section" structure.
- Use natural cross-marketing sections that connect related needs.
- Let services appear through problems, outcomes, bundles, proof points, and pathways.
- Use pricing or plan teasers carefully, with deeper pricing handled on dedicated pricing/service pages.
- Keep customer-care actions pointed to the portal without turning the homepage into a login screen.

## Cross-Marketing Philosophy
Cross-marketing should feel conversational and natural, not like a compressed sitemap.

On the main-site, cross-marketing can connect services through business outcomes and related customer needs.

Inside the portal, cross-marketing must be much lighter:
- Introduce only one idea at a time.
- Spark curiosity without explaining an entire service.
- Link back to the main website.
- Avoid predictable service sections.
- Avoid introducing every service.
- Avoid pricing tables.
- Avoid service cards.
- Avoid duplicating service pages.
- Never compete with login, invoice, support, ticket, checkout, or account-management workflows.

## Business Priority
Cross-marketing opportunities should generally follow this long-term revenue priority:
1. Hosting & Cloud
2. Website & Software Development
3. Marketing & Branding
4. ICT Services

This is a business priority, not a homepage section order. It should guide which opportunities receive emphasis when a natural prompt or pathway is needed.

## Approved Visual Rhythm
Use a clear homepage rhythm:
1. Fade blue
2. White
3. Strong blue

Repeat this rhythm thoughtfully through the page so sections feel connected but not monotonous.

CTA sections should not merge visually into the footer. The final CTA needs its own spacing, contrast, and boundary before footer content begins.

## Recommended Flow

### Hero
Purpose:
Immediately communicate trust, modern ICT capability, and the breadth of Zenvik's technology services.

CTA direction:
- Primary: consultation, quote, or service discovery
- Secondary: explore services or view packages
- Portal link: subtle customer access only

### Trust and Capability Signals
Show credibility through:
- Security and reliability
- Support promise
- Delivery quality
- Technology competence
- Business outcomes

### Cross-Marketing Service Stories
Instead of one generic service grid, combine related needs:
- Launch and grow online: websites, hosting, domains, SSL, email
- Run smarter operations: custom systems, portals, automation, AI
- Reach customers better: branding, marketing, analytics, content
- Strengthen infrastructure: networking, support, security, ICT

### Focused Service Highlights
Use deeper highlights for selected services without making any single category dominate.

### Proof, Process, and Confidence
Include delivery process, project previews where real, client proof where real, guarantees, legal clarity, support expectations, and FAQs.

### Final CTA
Close with a broad invitation to start a project, request a quote, or speak with Zenvik. Keep this section visually separate from the footer.

## Footer
Footer should include:
- Services
- Company links
- Legal links
- Contact information
- Portal link
- Social links where available
